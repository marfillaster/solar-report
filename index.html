Executive Summary

The system is already strong. The main win is charging the EV earlier.

Over the first three months analyzed, the system showed a clear upward seasonal trend: self-sufficiency improved from 60% in January to 77% in March, while projected annual bill savings reached about PHP 105,981. The biggest optimization opportunity is shifting EV charging into the late-morning solar window, especially from 10:00 to 13:00.

What Stood Out

Three high-signal takeaways

  • The base household load is well matched to the 6.5 kWp array on non-EV days.
  • EV charging is the main driver of afternoon and evening grid import.
  • The battery is healthy and materially improves the value of exported daytime energy by shifting it into evening self-consumption.

Top Recommendation

Shift EV charging to 10:00-13:00

EV days averaged 17.5 kWh of grid import compared to only 5.9 kWh on non-EV days. Charging earlier would align the heaviest EV load with stronger PV output and a fuller battery.

Estimated impact: PHP 3,200-5,300 per year in added savings.
